# Online JP Witnessing & Queensland Digital Licence Guide

---

## PART 1 — GETTING YOUR STAT DEC WITNESSED ONLINE (No leaving the house)

### Option A — JP in the Community (FREE, Queensland Government)

Queensland has a network of JPs who can witness documents. Some offer online/video witnessing.

**Steps:**
1. Go to: **www.qld.gov.au/law/legal-mediation-and-justice-of-the-peace/jp-and-cdec-register**
2. Search for JPs in your area who offer "online" or "video" witnessing
3. Contact them directly to arrange a video call (Zoom, FaceTime, Teams)
4. During the video call, you show your ID and sign the document while they watch
5. You post the signed document to them, they countersign and post back
   — OR —
   Some JPs accept electronic signatures under Queensland's COVID-era reforms (check current rules)

**JP Hotline:** 1300 301 147 (Queensland Courts)

---

### Option B — JP Online Services

Several organisations offer online JP witnessing services:

**JP Online (jpol.com.au)**
- Video witnessing available
- Small fee may apply
- Book an appointment online

**LawConnect / LawPath**
- Online document witnessing
- lawconnect.com.au

**Australia Post (in-person, but widespread)**
- Many Australia Post outlets have JPs available
- Call 13 13 18 to find your nearest
- Some offer appointments to reduce wait time

---

### Option C — Electronic Witnessing (Queensland)

Under Queensland's *Justice and Other Legislation (COVID-19 Emergency Response) Amendment Act 2020* and subsequent legislation, electronic witnessing of statutory declarations may be permitted. Check current status at:
- **www.legislation.qld.gov.au**
- Or call Queensland Courts: **1300 301 147**

---

### What to Have Ready for JP Witnessing:
- Completed Stat Dec (all fields filled except signature)
- Photo ID (driver's licence, passport, or Medicare card + another document)
- Exhibit A (email bounce notification — screenshot or printout)
- Any other exhibits referenced in the declaration

## PART 2 — QUEENSLAND DIGITAL DRIVER'S LICENCE

### What is it?
The Queensland Digital Licence is a digital version of your driver's licence stored in the **Service Queensland app** on your iPhone. It is legally accepted as proof of identity and age at most venues and for most purposes.

### Step-by-Step Setup:

**Step 1 — Download the app**
- App Store: search "Service Queensland"
- Or go to: **www.service.qld.gov.au/digital-licence**

**Step 2 — Create or log in to your MyAccount**
- You need a Queensland Government MyAccount
- Create at: my.account.qld.gov.au
- Use your email address

**Step 3 — Verify your identity**
- You'll need your physical licence number (or last known licence number)
- Plus Medicare card or passport for identity verification
- The app uses facial recognition to confirm it's you

**Step 4 — Add your licence**
- Once verified, your digital licence appears in the app
- It shows your photo, name, DOB, licence class, and expiry

---

### If Your Licence is Suspended (SPER):

**Check your licence status:**
- Go to: **www.tmr.qld.gov.au** → "Check your licence status"
- Or call TMR: **13 23 80**

**If suspended by SPER:**
1. Your licence is suspended until SPER fines are paid OR a payment arrangement is made OR fines are successfully reviewed
2. Filing a Stat Dec and requesting review PAUSES enforcement action while under review
3. Call SPER: **1300 304 702** and say: "I am filing a Statutory Declaration for review of my fines. I am requesting a hold on enforcement action while my review is processed."
4. SPER must acknowledge your review request
5. Once fines are resolved, TMR reinstates your licence — usually within 24-48 hours of SPER notifying TMR

**If you need to drive before resolution:**
- Apply for a Work/Medical Hardship Licence through TMR
- Grounds: caring responsibilities, medical appointments, essential travel
- Form: F3520 — Application for special hardship order
- Magistrates Court process — consider contacting Legal Aid Queensland: **1300 65 11 88**

---

### Lost Physical Licence:

If you can't find your physical licence:
1. Check your licence status online first (tmr.qld.gov.au)
2. If valid, set up the digital licence (steps above) — this works even without the physical card
3. To replace a lost physical licence: tmr.qld.gov.au → "Replace your licence" → $16.80 fee
4. Can be done fully online if your details are current
